The Sun's Nearest Star

Although not visible to the eye, Proxima Centauri is the closest known star to our sun, (only) 4.22 light years away! We cannot see Proxima Centauri because it is what astonomers call a "red dwarf star;" compared to other stars it is cold and very dim.

Proxima Isn't Alone

Proxima Centauri is a part of the Alpha Centauri star system, three stars that are caught within eachother's gravitational field, with Alpha Centauri A and Alpha Centauri B. It's membership in the star system is not certain, as it is one fifth of a light year away from the other two stars.

Proxima B

Astronomers have recently discovered the planet Proxima B rotating Proxima Centauri within the habitable zone of Proxima Centauri, meaning that liquid water could exist on the surface. But, it was discovered that Proxima B does not have an Earth-like atmosphere, so there is likely no life.
